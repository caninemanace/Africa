About Africa

From the Sahara to the Cape, Africa is rich in history, culture, and biodiversity. This page gives an overview of regions, peoples, languages, and communities that make the continent unique.

Explore the heritage, resilience, and innovation across cities and rural communities. Learn about traditions, modern achievements, and the challenges being met by local initiatives and international partnerships.

Regions & Landscapes

Sahara & Sahel

Expansive deserts, resilient communities, and unique ecosystems that span North and West Africa.

Savannahs & Grasslands

Home to diverse wildlife and agricultural communities supporting local economies.

Coastlines & Islands

Rich coastal fisheries, trade history, and vibrant island cultures from the Mediterranean to the Indian Ocean.

Culture & People

A mosaic of languages, traditions, music and art. Community and family ties are strong, and creativity fuels many modern innovations across the continent.

History Snapshot

Africa's history stretches from ancient civilizations such as Ancient Egypt and Carthage to powerful medieval kingdoms like Mali and Great Zimbabwe. The modern era includes complex colonial histories and inspiring independence movements in the 20th century that shaped today's nations.

  • Ancient trade routes connected inland regions with coastal ports.
  • Rich oral traditions preserved histories across generations.
  • Independence movements in the 20th century led to modern nation-states.

Statistics & Development

Economic growth varies widely across countries. Many economies are diversifying into technology, services, and manufacturing while also addressing gaps in infrastructure and healthcare.

Youth

Median age is under 20 in several countries — a demographic opportunity for innovation.

Urbanization

Rapid urban growth is reshaping cities and creating new opportunities for services and jobs.

Renewables

Large potential for solar and wind projects to expand energy access sustainably.
